WHY

Why was Michael Jackson the King of Pop?

Michael Jackson earned his place as the King of Pop through his exceptional talent, groundbreaking performances, and unparalleled influence in the music industry. His solo career, highlighted by albums like “Off the Wall” and “Thriller,” solidified his position as a force to be reckoned with.

In addition to his fame, Jackson is the most successful entertainer of all time, the most depicted cultural figure of the century, has the biggest selling album of all time (Thriller), the most watched interview ever, the most watched music video of all time,[148] along with being the most awarded artist in history.

Beat It

"Beat It" is a song by American singer-songwriter Michael Jackson. It was written and composed by Jackson and produced by Quincy Jones and co-produced by Jackson. Jones encouraged Jackson to include a rock song on the album. It includes a guitar solo by Eddie Van Halen.

Thriller

"Thriller" is a funk song featuring a repeating synthesizer bassline and lyrics and sound effects evoking horror films. It was produced by Quincy Jones and written by Rod Temperton, who wanted to write a theatrical song to suit Jackson's love of film. It has been named the greatest music video of all time.

Release Date

January 23, 1984
